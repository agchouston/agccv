About the Political Action Committee

Since 1977, Houston AGC PAC has been the nonpartisan political action committee (PAC) of AGC Houston. The PAC supports candidates running for elective office who, regardless of party affiliation, understand the construction industry and the specific needs, interests and concerns of AGC member companies and their employees.

Talking Points

Supporting our PAC ensures that AGC Houston continues to:

  • be a leading voice of the commercial construction industry
  • have strong relationships at all levels of government
  • positively impact legislative issues affecting our industry
  • support the campaigns of people who understand and support our issues - Early Voting is happening now through Friday, Feb. 25th. Primary Election Day is next Tuesday, March 1st.

Local successes attributed to the chapter's political involvement include:

  • Access to the Mayor and key City Council offices
  • Board member representation on Mayor's local government committees
  • Board member representation on the Mayor's permitting task force
  • Access to Harris County officials and key offices

2021 Legislative Victories

  • Design Defects (SB 219): This bill makes contractors no longer liable for architectural/engineering design defects unless the contractor knew about them and failed to disclose them.
  • Statute of Repose for Public Work (HB 3069): This legislation shortens the time period for suing for design/construction defects ("statute of repose") for public work from 10 years (plus 2 years to sue) to 8 years (plus 1 year to sue).
  • Uniform General Conditions for K-12 Contracts (SB 338): This bill adds two positions to the uniform general conditions (UGCs) statutory workgroup of the Texas Facilities Commission and allow for the collaborative development of UGCs for adoption by school districts for construction contracts.
  • Pandemic Protections - Construction Remains Essential (SB 968): Among various items, this legislation prohibits local officials from issuing orders during a pandemic disaster that would shut down commercial construction.
  • Commercial General Liability Coverage Gap (HB 2416): This bill fills a coverage gap (In Re Nalle case) by authorizing a person to recover attorney's fees as compensatory damages for breach of a construction contract.
  • Contingency Fee Clean-Up Bill (SB 1821): This legislation specifies that a contingent fee contract includes an amendment to a contingent fee contract if the amendment changes the scope of representation or may result in the filing of a lawsuit or the amending of a petition in an existing lawsuit.
  • Lien Law Modernization (HB 2237): This legislation streamlines the current Texas lien statutes, remove redundant provisions, address various ambiguities and provide for specific statutory forms for notice. AGC-TBB is publishing an updated lien law book in the coming weeks.

2019 Legislative Victories - We addressed construction defects litigation abuse by passing the following legislation:

  • Right to Repair (HB 1999): This legislation allows those involved in designing or constructing a public building or public work a chance to inspect and repair alleged defects before being sued.
  • School Construction Defects (HB 1734): This bill requires school districts to spend funds to actually repair construction defects when those funds are awarded from a verdict or settlement resulting from the defects.
  • Government Contingent Fee Contracting (HB 2826): This legislation, advanced by Texans for Lawsuit Reform, requires local governments to use a public and fully transparent process to retain contingent-fee lawyers while shifting oversight to the AG.

PAC vs. LAF Funds

Ways to Give

Political Action Committee (PAC)
Individual Funds

Usage
To support pro-construction candidates for office and other political committees.

We DO NOT contribute PAC funds to federal candidates (President/VP, Senate, House).

We DO contribute PAC funds to state and local candidates (state, county, city).

  • There is no contribution limit.
  • Contributions are not tax deductible.
  • Donated funds may be from:
    • an individual
    • any of the following with no corporation or incorporated members or partners:
      • LLP
      • LP
      • LC
      • LLC
      • PC
      • LTD
  • Funds from corporations (INC or CORP) cannot be accepted as PAC donations.
  • Checks should be payable to: HOUSTON AGC PAC
  • We CANNOT accept:
    • Contributions from foreign nationals. Foreign nationals are persons who are neither citizens of the United States nor lawfully admitted for permanent residence. "Green Card" holders are permitted to contribute.
    • Contributions in the name of another. You may contribute from your own personal funds (or the funds of your spouse/partner, in the case of a joint contribution).

Legislative Advocacy Fund (LAF)
Corporate Funds

Usage
To support AGC Houston's Government Affairs/Advocacy activities.

We DO NOT contribute LAF funds to any candidates for office or any political cause/organization.

  • There is no contribution limit.
  • Contributions are not tax deductible. (22% for 2022)
  • Donated funds may be from:
    • an individual
    • any entity (including corporations)
